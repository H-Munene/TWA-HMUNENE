Who Are we

As a non-governmental organization, we are committed to fostering transparency and accountability in government development initiatives. By rigorously monitoring and evaluating government projects, we aim to ensure that public resources are utilized effectively and efficiently for the benefit of all citizens. Our work is guided by the principles of good governance, participatory democracy, and sustainable development. Specific Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) aligned with our mission include: SDG 16: Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions: We advocate for accountable institutions and the rule of law to ensure that development projects are implemented fairly and transparently. SDG 17: Partnerships for the Goals: We collaborate with government agencies, civil society organizations, and the private sector to promote effective partnerships and achieve shared development objectives. SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth: We work to ensure that development projects create jobs, improve livelihoods, and contribute to economic growth. SDG 10: Reduced Inequalities: We strive to address inequalities and promote social justice through our advocacy and monitoring efforts. By shedding light on the progress or shortcomings of government development projects, we empower citizens to hold their elected officials accountable and demand better outcomes for their communities.
